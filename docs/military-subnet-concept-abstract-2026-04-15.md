### Section 1: Technical Concept Title

**AI-Driven Personalized Nutritional Supplementation for Warfighter Readiness: A Dynamic, Data-Integrated Platform for Force Health Protection**

---

### Section 2: Problem Statement

Current military nutrition programs rely on population-level dietary reference intakes and standardized combat rations that cannot account for individual variance in metabolism, biomarker status, physical demand, or operational tempo. The result is a readiness gap: warfighters may meet caloric targets but remain deficient in micronutrients critical to cognitive function, stress resilience, sleep quality, and recovery — deficiencies that are invisible until they manifest as degraded performance or increased injury risk.

Reactive healthcare intervention at that point is costly. A proactive, individualized nutritional support system — one that continuously adapts to a warfighter's actual biomarker profile — represents a force multiplier with measurable impact on readiness, retention, and long-term health outcomes.

---

### Section 3: Proposed Solution

**Ones AI** is a commercially deployed AI-powered personalized supplement platform that:

1. **Conducts structured AI-driven health consultations** — a conversational AI practitioner gathers health history, goals, symptoms, and lifestyle factors through natural dialogue, eliminating the need for specialized personnel or lengthy intake processes

2. **Integrates objective biomarker data** — the platform ingests lab results (blood panels, hormone profiles) and wearable biometric data (HRV, sleep stages, recovery scores) from devices including Fitbit, Oura Ring, and Whoop, creating a continuously updated physiological baseline

3. **Generates custom supplement formulas** — an evidence-based AI model selects from a validated catalog of 200+ pharmaceutical-grade ingredients, determining precise dosages within clinically established ranges based on the individual's profile

4. **Validates all formulas against safety constraints** — backend validation enforces dosage limits, contraindication checks, and ingredient interaction rules before any formula is confirmed; no formula is generated outside the validated catalog

5. **Adapts dynamically over time** — as new lab results or biometric data are ingested, the formula adjusts; the system tracks formula versions and maintains a longitudinal health record

6. **Delivers through a scalable SaaS architecture** — the platform is web and mobile accessible, designed to onboard hundreds of users simultaneously without per-user clinical staff overhead

---

### Section 4: Military Application

The Ones platform can be deployed as a **force health protection tool** for any unit or installation with the following workflow:

1. Warfighter completes initial AI consultation (~15 minutes, no clinical staff required)
2. Existing lab data (from periodic physicals or special operations screening panels) is uploaded directly into the platform
3. Wearable device data syncs automatically for continuous monitoring
4. A personalized supplement protocol is generated, reviewed by a supervising health officer if required, and fulfilled through DLA's existing pharmaceutical/supplement supply chain or a designated third-party manufacturer
5. Monthly re-evaluations update the formula based on current biomarker status and performance feedback

**Measurable readiness outcomes:**
- Reduction in micronutrient deficiencies identified through follow-up lab panels
- Improved HRV and sleep quality metrics from wearable data (quantifiable, automated)
- Reduction in sick call frequency for targeted deficiency-related complaints
- Warfighter-reported improvements in energy, focus, and recovery (validated survey instruments)

**Scale:** The platform is designed to support company- to brigade-level deployments (50–5,000 users) without proportional increases in clinical staffing.

### Section 6: IP and Data Rights

Ones AI intends to retain ownership of its proprietary AI models, formulation algorithms, and ingredient validation system under DFARS 252.227-7013 (rights in technical data) and 252.227-7014 (rights in computer software). The government would receive a license limited to the scope of the awarded contract. No proprietary formulation logic or AI model weights would be transferred as deliverables; only the operational platform and its outputs (user formulas, biomarker reports) constitute deliverables.

Warfighter health data would be stored in a HIPAA-compliant architecture with encryption at rest and in transit. The platform does not share individual health data with third parties. A DISA STIG compliance assessment and FedRAMP readiness review can be initiated during Phase 1 if required for deployment.
